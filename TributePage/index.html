George Scialabba

A "literary monk" whose book reviews not only point others down thought-provoking intellectual pathways but also blaze new trails through forboding political wilderness.

Scialabba's most recent collection of essays and reviews, Slouching Towards Utopia (2018) takes readers from the insitutional criticism of Ivan Illich all the way towards a personal vision of political utopia.

Some of his more notable pieces of writing include:

  • His first published review, The Political Economy of Human Rights is a passioned call to book reviewers and readers alike, maybe we should listen to this Noam Chomsky character.
  • What is the most horrifying thing imaginable? In Message from Room 101 Scialabba has a cogent answer, worth reading about if not to relate with, than at least to exercise one's empathy.
  • Few modern intellectuals have been given as high a status as Christopher Hitchens. In a farewell tribute to the great writer and thinker, Scialabba both honors the man's intellect and scrapes away some of the mythos.
  • "How bad is it?" So begins Scialabba's review of one of the most dystopian surveys of modern American Politics, Dark Ages America, written by the ever-ominous and enlightening, Morris Berman.
  • The intellectual I am most grateful for being turned on to, Ivan Illich is an enigma. This literary synopsis was the jumping off point for my own exploration of Illich's work and I am endlessly grateful for it.
  • This review of Matthew Crawfords book, The World Beyond Your Head, receives honorable mention here because while I found the review after reading the book, Scialabba's insight wanted me to run straight back to the library to check it out again.

Scialabba has a trove of additional work that I encourage you to check out. It can be found at his webpage along with links to his books and other miscellaneous thoughts and entries.
